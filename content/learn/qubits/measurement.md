---
title: Measurement
description: Measure a qubit repeatedly and watch probabilities turn into definite outcomes.
---

Measurement is the moment quantum uncertainty becomes a classical answer. When you measure a qubit in the computational basis, you always get 0 or 1 — never a fraction.

For |+⟩, each individual measurement is random, but over many repetitions the counts approach 50/50. That is the signature of superposition: random single outcomes, predictable statistics.

Prepare |+⟩ and click **Measure** repeatedly. The histogram fills in, and the Bloch view shows where the state lives on the sphere before collapse.
