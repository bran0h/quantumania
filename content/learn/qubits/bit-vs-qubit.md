---
title: Bit vs Qubit
description: Compare a classical bit with a qubit and see why quantum information is different.
---

A classical computer stores information in **bits**. Each bit is unambiguous: it is either 0 or 1, like a light switch that is off or on.

A **qubit** can also be prepared in |0⟩ or |1⟩, but it can also exist in combinations such as |+⟩, where neither outcome is definite until you measure it.

Try the panel below: flip the classical bit and switch the qubit between |0⟩, |1⟩, and |+⟩. Notice how the classical bit is always exactly one value, while the qubit can represent something richer.
